Launching into EIT Management

by Doug Millward

In this module we will discuss the emerging discipline of Enterprise IT and how it encompasses many modern trends including Automation, IOT and Digital Transformation. We will also discuss how EIT compares with Industry X.0 - and the technologies that underpin both trends. It is an exciting and disruptive time for business computing – and the recent changes in technologies and business processes has led to a wider, more all-encompassing concept, known as Enterprise IT.

Learning Objectives

  • Review what is meant by EIT and how it differs from business computing and Industry 4/5.0.
  • Discuss various techniques and approaches including the increased use of automation and how digital information is changing the face of Enterprise IT.
  • Review emerging trends in EIT including the effect of Web 3.0, Artificial Intelligence and the increasing risks of Cyber Security threats.

Outcomes

  • Critical appraisal of ideas and key concepts in IT service management, identified as key knowledge areas, including principles around enterprise architecture, strategy and governance, change management, security, interoperability, operations and support.
  • Critical evaluation of the literature and current developments and challenges in the field of IT service management (QMC).
  • Systematic and thorough understanding of relevant tools and techniques used to analyse and solve problems.
  • Systematically identify and manage scientific and technical risk and uncertainty associated with IT service management and its applications.
  • Application of the techniques and conventions of postgraduate research to a significant research project in the field of IT service management.

Reflection

The Enterprise IT module has significantly enhanced my understanding of how technology integrates with contemporary business practices, particularly through the lens of Automation, the Internet of Things (IoT), and Digital Transformation. I gained valuable insights into the distinctions between Enterprise IT and traditional business computing, as well as the implications of emerging trends like Web 3.0 and Artificial Intelligence. This experience underscored the importance of aligning technology strategies with organizational goals while also highlighting the critical need for robust cybersecurity measures in an increasingly complex landscape. The module's structured yet flexible approach allowed me to engage deeply with the material, ultimately preparing me to navigate the challenges and opportunities within IT service management effectively.
