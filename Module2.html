    YASAI Simulation

    YASAI is a simulation add-on for Microsoft Excel, designed for teaching Monte Carlo simulation to beginning students. It is distinguished by its ease of use, user-friendly interface, and ability to generate random variables from various probability distributions using spreadsheet functions. YASAI allows users to specify model outputs using the SIMOUTPUT function and offers flexible scenario management with the PARAMETER function, allowing for testing different parameter combinations. While not as feature-rich as some commercial packages such as @Risk or Crystal Ball, YASAI's simplicity, accessibility, and formula-driven design make it a valuable tool for teaching basic simulation concepts.

Security and Risk Management

by Doug Millward

Security and risk are increasingly being treated as inseparable as enterprises move away from traditional siloed views of business towards more integrated, enterprise solutions. In this module, we will look at the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. We will review the various methods of risk assessment – looking at both qualitative and quantitative methods. We will discuss a number of more traditional risk models (such as STRIDE, DREAD, and Attack Trees) as well as reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We will also review common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. Finally, we will discuss some of the key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management.

Learning Objectives

  • Discuss the difference between qualitative and quantitative risk assessments.
  • Describe how to carry out both kinds of assessments.
  • Explain what is meant by and how to create threat models.
  • Demonstrate how to create quantitative risk models.
  • Discuss and design DR solutions.

Outcomes

  • Describe when to use qualitative and quantitative risk assessments.
  • Create threat models for various scenarios.
  • Create quantitative risk models.
  • Design and explain DR strategies and solutions.
