Module 0 – Induction Computing

by Cathryn Peoples (April 2024)

This module is designed to provide students with a comprehensive introduction to KOL and the University of Essex Online, including its virtual learning environment (VLE), Moodle, and the various library resources available to support academic study. Module 2 – Security and Risk Management

by Doug Millward (July 2024)

Security and risk are increasingly being treated as inseparable as enterprises move away from traditional siloed views of business towards more integrated, enterprise solutions. In this module, we will look at the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. We will review the various methods of risk assessment – looking at both qualitative and quantitative methods. We will discuss a number of more traditional risk models (such as STRIDE, DREAD, and Attack Trees) as well as reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We will also review common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. Finally, we will discuss some of the key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management.

Module 6 – Cloud Operations and Management

by Zeeshan Siddiqui (July 2025)

Cloud computing has evolved from a primarily cost-saving approach to a transformative force shaping digital business models. Through this module, Cloud Operations and Management, I have gained a comprehensive understanding of the cloud ecosystem—covering fundamental service models, deployment options, and advanced architectures that include artificial intelligence (AI) and automation. Initially, I perceived the cloud as primarily offering scalable storage and computing power. However, over twelve weeks, I learned to see it as a strategic enabler for innovation, resilience, and sustainability across various industries. This e-Portfolio critically reflects on my learning journey, emphasising key insights, technical skills, and real-world applications. Each section demonstrates how theoretical knowledge was translated into practical skills and how these experiences have reshaped my view of modern IT operations.
